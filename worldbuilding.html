The Walls

  • The entire kingdom is surrounded by physical and magical walls. The physical walls between cities are thiner and shorter than the outermost walls. This allows for air and ground travel between cities without seeing anything beyond the kingdom. The outermost walls are about thirteen miles (20.9 km) in height, and the inner cities' walls are about 6 miles (9.65 km) in height.
  • The physical walls seemingly extend infinitely underground, and attempts to find the end in the past has lead to devastating casualties.
  • All air travel has a maximum altitude before simply being unable to work. The maximum altitude is the height of the outermost walls.
  • The magical walls are best seen from the sky. A iridescent shimmer can be seen in certain lighting from above as it makes a seemingly dome shape across the kingdom. Magic users and philosophers have hypothesized that the day and night cycle is simulated by the magical barrier. Records show all types of weather patterns to have occurred in the past, but in recent centuries extreme weather changes have come far and few between.

The Soil

  • Due to the manner in which the kingdom was formed, there has always been a startling lack of a fertile soil. In the beginning, all fertile soil was blessed by Emer and given to the Royal Family to manage. Only this blessed soil has the capacity to grow life.
  • In modern times, having this soil has been a sign of great wealth or an indication of close ties with the Royal Family.
  • When The Garden was being constructed, the Family donated blessed soil to the temple. Over the centuries an enormous tree has grown from it, and has become a symbol of a potential future.

The Water

  • Other than the blessed soil, water is the second most regulated resource in Eora. Initially it was also given to the Royal Family to manage, but as Eora grew the Family handed the responsibility to the Head Councilman of Water and their respective boards.
  • All water is sourced from the Trench. The Trench is a chasm of groundwater the circles around Hollyhock. The only time the public has access ti the Trench is on Blessings of the Waterbearer. On this day (that occurs once every eight years), the Trench is opened in a feat of engineering, and the Waterbearer pours rain only into the chasm.
  • Water is mostly used for hydroponic farming. With the list of crops being reduced to only potatoes, wheat and rice overtime.

The Everyday

Food

  • The crops that remain are only potatoes, wheat and rice. Rice is expensive, and is mostly seen in upper-class districts and cities. Wheat and potatoes are more plentiful and cheap, and are a staple in most dishes.
  • Other produce, spices, condiments, etc. are now chemically manufactured.
  • Meat and poultry are plentiful and are also a staple in most dishes.
  • Drinkable water is seen as a symbol of wealth, and is expensive to purchase pure. Most people drink milk (or diluted milk), manufactured juices or alcohol.

The Structures

  • Buildings and structures dating back to the beginning of Eora were made primarily of wood. As the kingdom grew, newer constructions were built from metal and concrete. Similar to drinkable water, having pure wooden fixtures is a sign of wealth and status.
